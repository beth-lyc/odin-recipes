Ingredients

  • 6-8 eggs
  • water
  • vinegar (optional: makes it easier to peel the eggs)

    • sauce

    • 1 part soy sauce
    • 2 part water
    • 1/2 chopped onion
    • 1 chopped green and red chilli
    • 5 cloves garlic
    • sesame seeds

Equipments

  • an airtight jar or container
  • a small pot (for boiling the eggs)
  • chopping board
  • knife

‚û§ Yields 6-8 servings


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Steps

  1. Bring a pot of water to boil, add vinegar
  2. Place 6-8 eggs in the pot and boil for 8 minutes (jammy yolks)
  3. While the eggs are boiling, let's prepare the sauce~
  4. Chop the onions, green and red chilli, garlic, and put them all in the airtight container with sesame seeds.
  5. Shock the boiled eggs under running cold water, then peel them
  6. Place the peeled eggs inside the airtight container, pour in water and soy sauce in a 2 to 1 ratio till it covers all eggs
  7. Cover the container and place it in the fridge overnight
  8. Cut the egg in half and serve with your favourite ramen!

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Q&A

How long does it last in fridge?

One to two weeks, two weeks max.
It tastes best within a week, and any time longer than that the eggs start to turn mushy.

Can I skip all the other fresh ingredients and just use soy sauce (and water)?

Absolutely! Feel free to trial and test what flavour you like best.
The soy sauce is the main ingredient that will give the eggs it's color, saltiness, and act as a preservative,
so as long as you don't skip that, you'll be fine!
