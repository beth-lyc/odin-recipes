Ingredients

  • 540mL sushi rice (short grain)
  • 540mL water

    • Sushi Vinegar

    • 6 tbsp rice vinegar
    • 3 tbsp sugar
    • 3/4 tbsp salt
    • kombu (optional)

Equipments

  • rice cooker
  • a small pot (for boiling the "sushi sauce")
  • a flat spatula
  • a large mixing bowl
  • a clean towel

‚û§ Yields 5-6 servings


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Steps

  1. Measure rice (3 rice cups), then wash in twice in cold water, drain with hand.
  2. Add 540mL of water to the rice and let sit for 25 minutes.
  3. Then in a small pot, add in the sushi vinegar ingredients - rice vinegar, sugar, salt (8:4:1) and kombu(optional).
  4. Heat the mixture so all ingredients melt but avoid bringing it to a boil. Set aside.
  5. Pop the rice into the rice cooker and press start.

    6. - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

  6. Once the rice is cooked, scoop the rice to a large mixing bowl, and avoid scraping burnt ones at the bottom.
  7. Pour the sushi vinegar over the spatula onto the rice, shifting the spatula left and right
    at the same time to evenly distribute the liquid onto the rice.
  8. Mix them by slicing the rice with the spatula, break down large chuncks to make sure they are all covered with the sushi vinegar.
  9. Set aside with a clean cloth covering the bowl to avoid the sushi rice getting stale and keep the steam in.
  10. There you go, authentic sushi rice that with go great with salmon, stir-fry pork with onion, or assembling it into rolled sushi!

Q&A

Can I use any rice for sushi rice?

Not really, you always want short grain rice to make sushi as short grain rice contains more starch and will help your sushi stick together!

So what's the difference of sushi rice and normal rice?

The type of rice grain, and sushi vinegar in sushi makes a lot of difference!
Short grain is used for sushi instead of long grains, which usually appears in Chinese cuisines.
The sushi vinegar also gives a faint combination of sweet, salty, and acidic flavour making the rice more appetizing.

How important it is to cover the bowl of mixed sushi rice with a cloth?

So...I did forget putting one on once I was making sushi. I didn't notice at first, and while I was busy rolling and assembling the sushi,
the rice goes from fresh steamy moisture filled warm rice into a pile of stale rice, it's still fine to eat, just a little bit of a waste if you know what I mean.
So summary of the above story is, always remember to cover your freshly made sushi rice with a clean cloth!
